Order of operations we recommend

  1. Land on the grid — home first; search if you are not in a scrolling mood.
  2. Click through — let MaisonLooks own the details; our card is only a preview.
  3. Interrogate the listing — size charts, stock flags, and fine print beat the tile’s vibe.
  4. Paste into Fishgoo — match their expected format; screenshots are backup, not primary input.
  5. Fund on fishgoo.com — cards, wallets, top-ups—whatever Fishgoo allows, inside their UI.
  6. Warehouse photos — approve or request retakes; this is your last cheap exit.
  7. Ship — choose a line, declare, pray to the customs gods responsibly.

What to double-check before you pay

  • Returns & cancellations — policies differ by seller and agent; read while exit is cheap.
  • Measurements — convert units; factory charts rarely match mall sizing.
  • Batch chatter — hype may reference an older run; verify on the live listing.
  • Declarations — you own truthful customs paperwork.
  • Landed math — item + fees + freight + possible VAT—sum it before you top up.

Before you blame the agent

  • Assuming English titles match factory reality — read measurements and material lines.
  • Chasing the lowest line price — shipping and service fees can erase the win.
  • Shipping hype items with the slowest line — balance risk and speed deliberately.
  • Ignoring seller processing times — pre-orders and holidays exist.
  • Using the same password everywhere — isolate your agent credentials.

Rough timeline (order → door)

Expect order → domestic transit → warehouse → QC → outbound → customs → delivery, with seasonal spikes. Domestic shipping is usually fast until it is not—factories miss dates too.
