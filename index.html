How China shopping agents work

Shopping agents (Taobao / Weidian / 1688 agents) sit between you and mainland sellers: they purchase domestically, receive your items at a warehouse in China, optionally photograph them for QC, then ship abroad when you create a parcel. This site is only for discovery—you always complete payment, disputes, and logistics on the agent you choose.

Typical ordering flow

  1. Find & verify the listing — Use the grid, full catalog, or MaisonLooks search. Read the seller page: photos, size chart, options, and return notes before you submit anywhere.
  2. Paste into your agent — On Kakobuy, Superbuy, Sugargoo, or any platform below, use their official “submit link” / converter. Pick variant, quantity, and add remarks (e.g. no branded packaging) if the form allows.
  3. Pay item + domestic leg — You pay the agent; they pay the seller. Costs usually include product price, seller→warehouse domestic freight, and later international shipping when you ship the parcel.
  4. Warehouse & QC — When the item arrives, many agents offer QC photos so you can confirm or request a return/exchange per that agent’s rules—before you approve international shipping.
  5. Consolidate & ship — Store multiple orders, then submit a parcel: choose a carrier line, insurance if offered, declare value for customs where required, pay freight, and track to your door.

Why people use agents instead of buying “direct”

Chinese marketplaces often require local payment methods, Mandarin support, and domestic-only returns. Agents bundle CNY checkout, warehouse storage, and global shipping lines in one workflow—especially useful if you order from several sellers and want one outbound box.

Proxy shopping guide: tips & safety

These habits reduce surprises whether you use a Kakobuy spreadsheet, a Superbuy spreadsheet, or any other agent—discovery is easy; the expensive mistakes happen at checkout and shipping.

Before you pay

  • Use the official domain only — Bookmark your agent’s real site. Ignore random “cheaper fee” links in DMs or unofficial apps.
  • Compare service fees — Photography, storage time, rehearsal shipping, and currency conversion differ by platform; read their fee page once so estimates make sense.
  • Size & batch language — Streetwear and sneakers often reference factory “batches.” Cross-check size charts; when in doubt, ask the seller via the listing or your agent’s message field.

After the item hits the warehouse

  • Review QC carefully — Zoom in on labels, stitching, and color. If something is off, open a ticket before you approve international shipment—after dispatch, options narrow.
  • Declare responsibly — Follow your country’s import rules; agents may ask you to set declared value and contents—honesty reduces seizure risk and aligns with local law.
  • Consolidate when it saves money — Shipping one heavy box is often cheaper per kilo than many small packets—if storage time allows.

Frequently asked questions

Does this site run my agent?
No. We are discovery-only. Use your agent’s official website for balances, QC, and shipping.
What is a China shopping agent?
A shopping agent buys from Chinese marketplaces on your behalf, stores goods at a warehouse in China, and arranges international delivery—common for Taobao, Weidian, Tmall and 1688 when you do not have a local payment method or address.
What is warehouse QC?
QC (quality check) photos are taken after your item arrives at the agent’s warehouse so you can verify it matches the listing before you pay for overseas shipping.
Is browsing this catalog free?
Yes. You pay when you place orders through an agent—not for scrolling this catalog.
What if a product link stops working?
Re-run search or image search, open the full catalog, or pick another card—seller URLs and inventory change often.
Which marketplaces do agents usually support?
Most agents support Taobao, Weidian, and 1688; many also support Tmall and linked item pages. Always paste the exact URL into your agent’s converter to confirm.
