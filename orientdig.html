What is OrientDig (the short version)

As a forwarding-style agent, OrientDig sits between you and domestic sellers—collecting parcels, shooting QC, staging consolidation. Use our grid to explore; use OrientDig’s dashboard to execute.

Order of operations we recommend

  1. Land on the grid — home first; search if you are not in a scrolling mood.
  2. Click through — let MaisonLooks own the details; our card is only a preview.
  3. Interrogate the listing — size charts, stock flags, and fine print beat the tile’s vibe.
  4. Paste into OrientDig — match their expected format; screenshots are backup, not primary input.
  5. Fund on orientdig.com — cards, wallets, top-ups—whatever OrientDig allows, inside their UI.
  6. Warehouse photos — approve or request retakes; this is your last cheap exit.
  7. Ship — choose a line, declare, pray to the customs gods responsibly.

What to double-check before you pay

  • Seller notes — read the fine print on pre-sale, processing time, and color variance.
  • Size tolerance — measure twice; factory tolerances are not boutique tolerances.
  • Version drift — “same link” can mean different factory weeks; ask if unsure.
  • Insurance — decide whether you need add-ons before the parcel leaves China.
  • Payment timing — know what triggers non-refundable charges on your agent.
Safety tip. Phishing loves agent keywords. Bookmark orientdig.com from a source you trust; never trust a “shortcut” spreadsheet that asks for passwords.

Before you blame the agent

  • Letting chats set your risk tolerance — verify on the listing and with OrientDig.
  • Ignoring return windows — they shrink once outbound shipping starts.
  • Mixing up color names — “black” and “off-black” are different arguments.
  • Underestimating holidays — China’s calendar moves your timeline.
  • Bookmarking phishing domains — verify orientdig.com character by character.

Rough timeline (order → door)
