You still make your own judgment about quality, sizing, and seller reputation—no automated guarantee— but you waste less time on 404s and “link expired” messages.

What is Kakobuy (the short version)

Kakobuy is a shopping agent: you pay them to purchase from Chinese sellers who may not ship overseas or accept your card. The agent receives the parcel domestically, photographs it for QC, stores it briefly, then forwards it internationally using a shipping line you pick. That model is why spreadsheets exist in the first place—people need a discovery phase (find the link, compare batches) separate from the execution phase (submit the link, approve QC, ship). This page only helps the first phase; Kakobuy’s own UI handles the second.

How to use this site, then buy on Kakobuy (or any agent)

  1. Start on the home grid — open the spreadsheet home, scroll, and let new rows load; or jump straight to search / image search if you already know what you want.
  2. Open the listing — click a card; MaisonLooks should open in a new tab with the full product context (variants, seller-facing copy, imagery).
  3. Read the listing carefully — confirm size charts, color names, batch notes, and whether the item is pre-order or in stock. If something is unclear, assume risk until you clarify with the seller path your agent provides.
  4. Copy what your agent needs — some flows want the full URL; others want a product ID. Follow whatever Kakobuy’s paste box or bookmarklet asks for.
  5. Pay on the agent — never send payment to random DMs; use the official balance or checkout on kakobuy.com.
  6. Review warehouse QC — when photos appear, zoom in on labels, stitching, and color accuracy before you approve international shipping.
  7. Choose a line and declare — speed vs cost vs customs risk varies by country; that advice belongs in agent help docs and your local experience, not on this page.

What to double-check before you pay

  • Return and cancellation rules — agents and sellers differ; read the fine print while you still can back out cheaply.
  • Size and measurements — convert units; do not assume US/EU labels match every factory chart.
  • Batch / version language — some items have multiple runs; community hype may refer to an older batch.
  • Declared value — you are responsible for truthful customs paperwork on the agent side.
  • Total landed cost — item + domestic fee + international freight + possible VAT—mentally sum them before you commit.
Safety tip. Phishing sites love popular agent names. Bookmark kakobuy.com from a source you trust and never enter passwords after clicking random “official spreadsheet” links in DMs.

Quick glossary

Agent
A service that purchases domestically in China and forwards internationally—Kakobuy is one example among many.
QC
Quality-control photos taken at the warehouse before you approve international shipment—not the same as marketing photos on a seller page.
W2C / finds
“Where to cop” / discovery slang: the hunt for a link before anyone commits to a cart.
Haul
A batch of items purchased and shipped together; discovery tools help you plan what belongs in the same parcel.
Domestic leg
Shipping from the Chinese seller to the agent’s warehouse—usually fast, but still subject to seller delays and holidays.
Service fee
What the agent charges to place the order, sometimes shown as a percentage or flat fee on top of the item price—check Kakobuy’s fee page for current rules.
Consolidation
Combining multiple warehouse items into one international parcel to save on volumetric shipping—handled in the agent UI after items arrive.
Rehearsal / packing
Optional step where the agent weighs and pre-packs to estimate freight; names vary by platform.

How to read a listing after you click through

The grid on our home page is intentionally lightweight: it helps you scan quickly. The MaisonLooks product page is where you should slow down. Look for multiple photos (not just the hero image), any mention of pre-sale vs in-stock, size charts with actual measurements—not only S/M/L—and whether color names match what you expect. If the listing mixes Chinese and English, assume the numeric measurements matter more than translated adjectives. When in doubt, screenshot the variant you want and keep it next to your agent order so you can compare later against warehouse QC.

Common mistakes (easy to avoid)

  • Assuming the spreadsheet row is still valid — always re-open the live listing the same day you pay.
  • Ignoring the size chart — factory charts differ from high-street brands; measure a garment you already own and compare flat lengths.
  • Rushing warehouse QC — blurry photos are a reason to ask for retakes before international shipping, not after.
  • Forgetting domestic + international fees — the item price is rarely the full landed cost.
  • Trusting DMs over official domains — support, payments, and refunds should happen inside the agent’s authenticated site.

Rough timeline (order → door)

No one can promise dates across every seller and country, but the sequence is usually: order placed → seller ships domestically → warehouse receives → QC photos → you approve → international line ships → customs in your country → last-mile delivery. Busy seasons (November, Chinese New Year) stretch every step. Track inside Kakobuy; if a step stalls, open a ticket there—this discovery site cannot see your parcel.

Customs, duties, and “will I pay VAT?”

Rules depend on where you live, what you declared, which carrier you used, and local enforcement—not on this guide. Many shoppers read their country’s import thresholds and discuss experiences in communities; your agent may offer declaration guidance, but you remain responsible for compliance. When in doubt, budget extra for duties and assume inspections can delay delivery.

Building a haul without a spreadsheet tab for every SKU

Old-school hauls were planned in tabs: shoes here, tees there. You can mimic that mentally: favorite items in your head, screenshots, or a private note—then paste links into Kakobuy in batches. Consolidation rewards planning: heavier items and oddly shaped boxes affect volumetric weight, so grouping compatible pieces can be cheaper than shipping five one-off impulse buys. Exact math lives in the agent’s rehearsal tools.

When this catalog is not enough

If you need dispute mediation, insurance claims, freight damage photos, or account recovery, you must use Kakobuy’s ticketing system or your payment provider—this site has no visibility into your orders. Likewise, if a product category is niche or the seller is private, you may still rely on forums and seller-specific guides; no single grid covers the entire Chinese internet.
